T H U K P A

P R E P 10 M I N | C O O K 25 M I N | Y I E L D 1

Ingredients

  • 50g Noodles
  • Assorted Vegetables (such as carrots, cabbage, bell peppers, and mushrooms)
  • 50g Protein (such as chicken, beef, or tofu)
  • 250ml Broth (vegetable or chicken)
  • 1 clove Garlic
  • 1 teaspoon Ginger
  • Chili peppers to taste (optional, for spice)
  • 1 stalk Scallions
  • 1 tablespoon Soy sauce
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • Coriander leaves (cilantro) for garnish

Directions

  • Prepare the broth: In a large pot, bring water or broth to a boil. Add aromatics like ginger, garlic, onions, and spices such as turmeric, cumin, and coriander. Let the broth simmer to develop flavors.
  • Cook the noodles: Add flat wheat noodles or rice noodles to the simmering broth. Cook until the noodles are al dente or according to package instructions.
  • Add vegetables and protein: Toss in chopped vegetables like carrots, cabbage, bell peppers, and protein sources like diced chicken, beef, or tofu. Allow them to cook until tender.
  • Season the soup: Adjust the seasoning of the soup with salt, pepper, soy sauce, and chili sauce according to taste preferences. You can also add a squeeze of lime juice for extra flavor.
  • Serve hot: Ladle the hot thukpa into bowls and garnish with fresh cilantro, chopped green onions, and a wedge of lime. Serve immediately and enjoy this comforting Tibetan noodle soup!
