Following are the Few Nepalese and Indian Cuisines With Detailed Recipes

Momos

Momos are steamed or fried dumplings filled with meat or vegetables, served with spicy dipping sauce, popular in both Nepal and India.

Dal Bhat

A traditional Nepali and Indian meal consisting of lentil soup (dal) served with steamed rice (bhat) and various vegetable or meat curries.

Thukpa

A hearty Tibetan noodle soup popular in Nepal and northeastern regions of India, made with vegetables, meat, and spices.

Rogan Josh

A flavorful Indian curry dish originating from Kashmir, made with tender pieces of meat (usually lamb or goat) cooked in a rich, aromatic gravy.

Saag Paneer

A North Indian dish made with spinach (saag) and paneer (Indian cheese), cooked with spices like cumin, garlic, and garam masala.

Chicken Tikka Masala

A popular Indian dish consisting of grilled chunks of chicken (tikka) cooked in a creamy, spiced tomato-based sauce (masala).

Sel Roti

A traditional Nepali rice flour doughnut, often enjoyed during festivals and special occasions, crispy on the outside and soft on the inside.

Samosa

A savory pastry originating from India, filled with spiced potatoes, peas, and sometimes meat, folded into a triangular shape.

Kheer

A traditional Indian and Nepali dessert made by simmering rice in milk until it thickens, flavored with cardamom, saffron, and nuts, served chilled or warm as a sweet treat after meals.
