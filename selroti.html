S E L R O T I

P R E P 30 M I N | C O O K 50 M I N | Y I E L D 20

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Rice flour
  • 1/2 cup All-purpose flour
  • 1 cup Ripe bananas, mashed
  • 1 cup Granulated sugar
  • 1/2 teaspoon Baking powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon Cardamom powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon Nutmeg powder
  • 1/4 teaspoon Salt
  • 1 cup Water (approximately)
  • Oil for deep frying

Directions

  • Prepare the batter: In a large mixing bowl, combine rice flour, all-purpose flour, sugar, mashed ripe bananas, and yogurt. Gradually add water while stirring continuously to form a thick, smooth batter. The batter should be thick enough to coat the back of a spoon.
  • Let the batter ferment: Cover the bowl with a clean cloth or plastic wrap and allow the batter to ferment overnight or for at least 6-8 hours at room temperature. Fermentation helps develop flavor and leavening agents naturally.
  • Heat oil for frying: Heat oil in a deep frying pan or kadhai over medium heat. The oil should be hot enough to fry the sel roti but not smoking hot.
  • Shape and fry the sel roti: Take a portion of the fermented batter and pour it into a piping bag or a plastic bottle with a small hole at the tip. Squeeze the batter into hot oil in a circular motion to form a ring-shaped roti. Alternatively, you can use a ladle to pour the batter into the oil and shape it manually.
  • Fry until golden brown: Fry the sel roti until they turn golden brown and crispy on the outside, flipping them halfway through the cooking process to ensure even browning. Use a slotted spoon to remove the fried sel roti from the oil and drain excess oil on paper towels.
  • Serve warm: Serve the sel roti warm as a snack or breakfast dish. They are delicious on their own or paired with tea, yogurt, or a side of spicy pickle. Enjoy the crispy exterior and soft interior of these traditional Nepali rice flour doughnuts!
