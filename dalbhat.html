D A L B H A T

P R E P 5 M I N | C O O K 20 M I N | Y I E L D 1

Ingredients

  • 1 cup of lentils (any variety such as red lentils, yellow lentils, or split pigeon peas)
  • 3 cups of water (for cooking lentils)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, grated
  • 1-2 green chilies, slit (adjust according to taste)
  • 1 medium tomato, chopped
  • 1 teaspoon of cumin seeds
  • 1 teaspoon of mustard seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon of turmeric powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon of red chili powder (adjust according to taste)
  • 1/2 teaspoon of ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon of garam masala
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 tablespoons of cooking oil or ghee
  • Fresh cilantro leaves for garnish (optional)

Directions

  • Prepare the lentils: Rinse and soak lentils (such as red lentils or yellow lentils) in water for about 30 minutes. In a pot, combine the soaked lentils with water, turmeric, salt, and any desired spices. Cook until the lentils are soft and mushy, adding more water if needed.
  • Cook the rice: Rinse rice (such as basmati rice or any local variety) thoroughly and cook it in a pot of boiling water until tender. Drain any excess water once the rice is cooked.
  • Prepare the tempering: In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee and add cumin seeds, mustard seeds, dried red chilies, and curry leaves. Allow the spices to splutter and release their aroma.
  • Add aromatics: Add finely chopped onions, garlic, ginger, and green chilies to the tempering. Saut√© until the onions turn golden brown and aromatic.
  • Combine lentils and tempering: Add the cooked lentils to the tempering mixture and stir well. Adjust the consistency by adding more water if needed. Simmer the dal for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld.
  • Serve hot: Serve the dal alongside the cooked rice on a plate or in bowls. Optionally, garnish with fresh cilantro leaves and a dollop of ghee. Enjoy your comforting and nutritious dal bhat meal!
