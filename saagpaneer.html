S A A G P A N E E R

P R E P 20 M I N | C O O K 40 M I N | Y I E L D 1

Ingredients

  • 150g Paneer (Indian cottage cheese), cubed
  • 2 tablespoons Ghee or vegetable oil
  • 1 Onion, finely chopped
  • 2 cloves Garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece Ginger, grated
  • 2 Green chilies, finely chopped (adjust to taste)
  • 1 teaspoon Cumin seeds
  • 1/2 teaspoon Turmeric powder
  • 1 teaspoon Ground coriander
  • 1/2 teaspoon Garam masala
  • 1/2 teaspoon Ground fenugreek (kasuri methi)
  • 200g Spinach, washed and chopped
  • 100g Mustard greens (optional), washed and chopped
  • 1 Tomato, finely chopped
  • 1/2 cup Water
  • 2 tablespoons Heavy cream (optional)
  • Salt to taste

Directions

  • Prepare the spinach: Wash fresh spinach leaves thoroughly and blanch them in boiling water for a few minutes until wilted. Immediately transfer the blanched spinach to a bowl of ice water to stop the cooking process. Drain the spinach and squeeze out excess water.
  • Puree the spinach: Place the blanched spinach in a blender or food processor and pulse until you achieve a smooth puree. Set aside.
  • Prepare the paneer: Cut paneer (Indian cottage cheese) into bite-sized cubes. Optionally, lightly fry the paneer cubes in a pan until golden brown for added flavor and texture.
  • Cook the aromatics: In a separate pan, heat oil or ghee over medium heat. Add finely chopped onions, ginger, and garlic to the pan and saut√© until the onions are soft and translucent.
  • Spice it up: Stir in ground spices such as cumin, coriander, turmeric, and garam masala into the onion mixture. Cook for a minute or until fragrant, being careful not to burn the spices.
  • Combine spinach and paneer: Add the pureed spinach to the pan with the spiced onion mixture. Stir well to combine and let it simmer for a few minutes to allow the flavors to meld together.
  • Add paneer: Gently fold in the cooked paneer cubes into the spinach mixture, ensuring they are evenly coated with the flavorful sauce.
  • Finish with cream (optional): For added richness and creaminess, you can stir in a splash of heavy cream or coconut milk into the saag paneer. Cook for another minute until heated through.
  • Garnish and serve: Garnish the saag paneer with a drizzle of cream and a sprinkle of dried fenugreek leaves (kasuri methi) for added aroma and flavor. Serve hot with naan bread or rice for a delicious and comforting meal!
